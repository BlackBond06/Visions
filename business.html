Securities Law

The attorneys that practice in our law firm's securities law practice group advise clients on securities issues and assist with preparing private placement memoranda and related private offering materials. Our law firmâ€™s approach is to thoroughly understand a client's business and future objectives so that our securities lawyers can better advise each client as well as to help ensure that any disclosure documents are accurate and complete.

At our law firm, the securities attorneys understand that there is a difference between simply assisting a client with drafting offering and disclosure documents and helping to guide the business owners and other key individuals to opportunities and relationships that can help them to achieve their goals; thus our attorneys are able to leverage their knowledge and contacts to identify people and businesses that can help clients to grow their businesses and expand their interests.

Although the principal focus of private offerings of securities is often compliance with, or meeting requirement for exemptions from, federal and state securities laws, securities laws are applicable in many other business transactions as well, including mergers and acquisitions and the formation of new ventures, including but not limited to corporations (S and C varieties), limited liability companies, limited and general partnerships, and joint ventures.

Public Offerings . We represent public companies and companies seeking to access public markets in initial public offerings (IPOs). When representing issuers, we typically become involved in all aspects of corporate and business planning and structuring so as to most effectively and efficiently provide the result that our clients are seeking.

Private Offerings . Some of our clients are active issuers of securities in private transactions which are commonly known as Regulation D 504, 505, or 506 offerings, or Regulation S offerings. Relating to these services, our attorneys also render advice regarding exemptions from federal and state securities registration laws and related regulatory matters. As is the case with public offerings, we also represent issuers, placement agents, and investors in private (exempt) transactions.

Transactions . The lawyers in our law firm's securities law practice group also perform a wide range of complex corporate transactions including mergers and acquisitions. As you would suspect, these lawyers apply their knowledge of securities laws and financing trends to assist clients in consummating these often difficult transactions in an innovative, cost-effective, and timely manner.

Business Entity formation

Our law firm provides counsel concerning all aspects of establishing, organizing capitalizing, and maintaining business entities, including (i) corporations; (ii) limited liability companies; (iii) limited and general partnerships; and joint ventures. The lawyers and staff that assist clients with entity formation can also assist with drafting and negotiating the agreements and instruments necessary to conduct your business successfully, such as:

  • Stockholder agreements
  • Partnership agreements,
  • Joint venture agreements,
  • Operating agreements,
  • Commercial sales and services agreements, and
  • Employment contracts

The attorneys at our firm are also able to guide a new or established business in all aspects of finance, business combinations, and other capital transactions, including:

  • Debt and equity financing,
  • mergers and acquisitions, and
  • stock and asset sales.

commercial Leasing

Madiba Chambers represents clients in all facets of retail, office, and industrial leasing and real property management. Our attorneys efficiently handle all aspects of the landlord/tenant relationship, including structuring, negotiating, and documenting all types of leases. Our services include but are not limited to:

Retail Leasing. Our attorneys have experience in the negotiation and documentation of all types of leases. Our experience includes the representation of developers for premises within regional malls, power centers, entertainment/lifestyle centers, community shopping centers, neighborhood shopping centers, and mixed-use projects in a variety of different lease transactions, including build-to-suit leases, reverse build-to-suit leases, pad leases, major tenant leases, and shop leases. In addition, our attorneys have significant experience representing both developers and retailers in ground leasing transactions.

Our attorneys also have experience representing developers and retailers selling and acquiring portions of land within a retail center and preparing corresponding purchase and sale agreements, reciprocal easement agreements, development agreements, and other agreements common to such transactions.

Office Leasing. Our attorneys also represent landowners and tenants in acquiring, developing, constructing, leasing, financing, and disposing of all types of office projects. Our experience includes negotiating and documenting office leases for landlords and tenants alike. In addition, we review office leases in connection with our representation of lenders and purchasers of major office projects and also have reviewed, analyzed, and developed form lease documentation for office projects.

Industrial Leasing. Our attorneys have experience representing major industrial developers and industrial tenants. We negotiate and draft leases on behalf of landlords and tenants of industrial space in multitenant and single-tenant industrial projects.

Finance

The attorneys at our law firm are proud to have represented a variety of traditional and nontraditional lenders, including banks, finance companies, private investment funds, and other entities, as well as developers and other borrowers, related to the financing of real property, business acquisitions or dispositions, receivables, and other assets. The capabilities of our finance lawyers are all-encompassing and include the initial structuring and closing of finance transactions, loan administration, workouts, and loan restructures.

  • Multistate/multiparcel cross-collateral transactions
  • Revolving lines of credit
  • Loans secured by ground leases
  • Loans secured by fractional ownership interest receivables
  • Loans secured by ground leases
  • Loans secured by crops and other farm products, and
  • The issuance of letters of credit.
